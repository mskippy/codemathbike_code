Overview

Big idea: Turn your plan into a complete, polished video and use structured feedback to decide how you will improve it before publishing.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Import and organize clips into a video timeline.
  • Apply basic editing: trimming, splitting, and pacing.
  • Balance audio and use simple colour correction.
  • Add titles, transitions, and lower thirds that support your story.
  • Use the RISE model to give and receive feedback.
  • Explain the changes you plan to make before final export.

You should already have: an approved pitch, script, storyboard, shot list, and your footage recorded.

Important: You will not hand in your video for this lesson. Instead, you will submit a 4.4 Editing Reflection (Word document). The finished video will be handed in once for the showcase in 4.5.

Choosing Your Aspect Ratio

For this project, you can choose the format that best fits your footage and story. Your video can be landscape (wider) or portrait (taller), as long as you use one of the standard sizes below.

  • Landscape (16:9): 1920 × 1080
  • Portrait (9:16): 1080 × 1920

If your device struggles to export at full HD, you may use a smaller version of the same shape (for example 1280 × 720 for landscape), but try to keep the aspect ratio the same.

Tip: In your editing app, set the project or sequence size before you start cutting so your video doesn’t get cropped or stretched.

Before You Edit

Do a quick pre-check before you start editing:

  • All your footage is saved in one project folder.
  • Any music you plan to use is original or Creative Commons.
  • You have your script and storyboard nearby.
  • You have headphones and enough focused time to work.

Activity A – Build Your Edit

Begin building the best version of your video (no separate rough/final labels). You will improve it later using RISE feedback and your own reflection.

  1. Create your project & sequence.
    • Open Premiere Rush, DaVinci Resolve, or Clipchamp.
    • Create a new project and import your clips.
    • Use a 1920×1080 sequence at 24 or 30 fps.
  2. Assemble the story.
    • Use your storyboard/script as a guide.
    • Bring in your best takes only.
    • Aim for a clear beginning, middle, and end.
  3. Clean up the pacing.
    • Cut out long pauses, “umms,” and repeated lines.
    • Remove shots that don’t help the story.
    • Watch for any parts that feel slow or confusing.
  4. Add titles & lower thirds.
    • Add a short title screen with your project name.
    • Optional: add lower thirds for names or labels.
    • Keep fonts readable and consistent.
  5. Basic polish.
    • Adjust audio so speech is clear and louder than music.
    • Use light colour correction so shots look similar.

When this version feels watchable from start to finish, you are ready for RISE feedback and your reflection.

Activity B – RISE Feedback Checkpoint

Use the RISE model to give and receive quick, focused feedback on your video. You will record this in your reflection for 4.4.

RISE model reminder:

  • R – Reflect: What is working well?
  • I – Inquire: What are you curious or unsure about?
  • S – Suggest: What specific changes could help?
  • E – Elevate: How could this be taken to the next level?
  1. Swap videos.
    Share your current version with your partner or small group (screen share or uploaded file).
  2. Watch once without pausing.
    View the video as if you were a regular audience member.
  3. Give RISE feedback.
    Each reviewer should provide at least:
    • 1 Reflect comment (strength).
    • 1 Inquire comment (question).
    • 1 Suggest comment (specific improvement).
    • 1 Elevate comment (next-level idea).

    Record this feedback in a place where you can use it for your 4.4 reflection (notes, screenshot, or copied text).

  4. Choose your next steps.
    After reading your feedback, pick 2–3 concrete changes to make in your edit. You will write about these in your reflection.

4.4 Editing Reflection – What You Submit

Create a Word document called LastName_4.4_Reflection.docx and answer the prompts below. This is the only file you submit for 4.4.

Part 1 – Editing Progress (3–5 sentences)

Explain:

  • What you have completed so far in your edit.
  • Which parts you think are working well.
  • What still needs improvement.

Part 2 – RISE Feedback (copy or summarize)

Include the key feedback you received from your peers. You can:

  • Type it under headings, for example:
    • Reflect: …
    • Inquire: …
    • Suggest: …
    • Elevate: …
  • Or paste a screenshot of your RISE feedback and label each part.

Part 3 – Planned Adjustments (2–3 specific changes)

Write about the changes you plan to make based on the feedback. Be specific. For example:

  • “I will reduce the background music volume during the interview.”
  • “I will trim the long wide shot at the beginning to tighten the pacing.”
  • “I will add a title card to clearly introduce the topic.”

Part 4 – Editing Skills Check

Rate each area as Strong, Okay, or Needs work and add one sentence about your biggest area of growth.

  • Pacing
  • Audio levels
  • Colour balance
  • Titles & lower thirds
  • Story clarity
  • Visual flow / shot order

Part 5 – Questions for the Teacher (optional)

List any questions you have or areas where you would like extra feedback before your final export.

Output for 4.4: Your completed 4.4 Editing Reflection Word document. You will continue improving your edit and submit the finished video in 4.5 Class Video Showcase.
