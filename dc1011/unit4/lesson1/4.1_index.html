Overview

Big idea: Understand how online videos use storytelling, platform conventions, and ethical choices to communicate with digital audiences.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Explain how creators tell stories for online audiences.
  • Identify how purpose, message, and tone shape a video.
  • Notice how platforms like YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram influence style and format.
  • Analyze a video using the PALE Framework (Purpose, Audience, Language/visual style, Ethics).

Output: A short PALE analysis on Padlet and a written or audio reflection.

1. Mini-Lesson – Why Video Matters Online

Video is one of the strongest communication tools today. Creators use visuals, audio, pacing, and platform-specific trends to capture attention and tell stories to digital audiences.

Key ideas

  • Storytelling for the scroll: Hooks, quick pacing, and captions help keep viewers engaged as they scroll.
  • Purpose, message, tone: Every video is made with an intention – to inform, entertain, persuade, inspire, or promote.
  • Platform influence:
    • YouTube: Longer, more structured, narrative-style content.
    • TikTok: Sound-driven, trend-based, with fast edits and visual emphasis.
    • Instagram: Aesthetic-focused, short bursts, stylized visuals.
  • Ethics: Representation, consent, copyright, and responsible messaging all matter when publishing video online.

2. Activity – PALE Video Analysis

Choose one short online video (TikTok, Reel, YouTube Short, ad, trailer, etc.) and analyze it using the PALE Framework.

PALE Framework

Use these questions to structure your analysis.

P – Purpose

  • What is the creator trying to do – inform, entertain, persuade, inspire, or promote something?
  • What clues in the video help you figure this out?

A – Audience

  • Who is the video designed for?
  • What clues show that? (tone, language, references, platform, style)

L – Language / Visual Style

  • How is the video edited? (cuts, pacing, transitions)
  • What visual choices do you notice? (lighting, colour, framing, text, emojis, filters)
  • How is audio used? (music, narration, sound effects)
  • What platform conventions or trends are being used?

E – Ethics

  • How are people represented in the video?
  • Do you think everyone shown has given consent to be filmed?
  • Is copyrighted material (music, clips, images) used appropriately?
  • Could the message be misleading, harmful, or spread misinformation?

Class Padlet – Share Your PALE Analysis

Post a summary of your PALE analysis on our class Padlet. Use one post per video and clearly label it with your name and the video title.

  • Include a short summary for each part of PALE (P, A, L, E).
  • Be specific – refer to moments, choices, or details in the video.
  • Read at least one other classmate’s post and notice similarities or differences.

3. Your Task – Short Reflection

After posting on Padlet, create a short reflection that captures your learning from this activity. Choose one format:

Option A – Written Reflection (150–250 words)

Your written reflection should include:

  1. The video you chose and why you selected it.
  2. Your PALE analysis (summarized) – the most important points from each part.
  3. One thing you learned about how video communicates with an online audience.

Option B – Audio Note (30–60 seconds)

Record a short audio note that follows the same structure:

  1. Introduce the video and why you chose it.
  2. Summarize your PALE analysis.
  3. Share one key insight about video communication.

Upload your audio file to Teams or OneDrive as directed.

Choose Your Challenge – Optional Extensions

Finished early or want to push your thinking further? Choose one challenge below. These are optional extensions to deepen your understanding of video communication.

Level 1 – Sharpen Your Analysis (entry-level)

Stay with the same video and dig a bit deeper.

  • Hook check: Re-watch the first 3–5 seconds and identify exactly what makes it attention-grabbing.
  • Caption audit: Look at the text/captions. How do they support or change the message?
  • Tone tweak: Imagine the same video with a different soundtrack. How would that change the tone?
Level 2 – Compare Two Videos (intermediate)

Choose a second video on a similar topic (or from the same creator).

  • Compare the purpose and audience of both videos.
  • Note one major difference in visual style (L) between the two.
  • Write 3–4 sentences or record a 20–30 second audio comparing how effective each video is.
Level 3 – Reimagine for a New Platform (stretch)

Take your chosen video and imagine it was designed for a different platform.

  • How would a YouTube version differ from a TikTok or Instagram Reel version?
  • List at least three changes you would make to pacing, visuals, and captions.
  • Optional: Sketch or describe a rough storyboard of your reimagined version.
