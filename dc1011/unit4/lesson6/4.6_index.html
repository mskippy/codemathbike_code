Overview

Big idea: Evaluate your own work and a peer’s work using structured criteria. This reflection shows your growth as a digital creator and helps finalize your assessment for Unit 4.

In this lesson, you will:

  • Reflect on your process from idea to final video.
  • Identify specific skills you developed in planning, filming, and editing.
  • Use the RISE model to evaluate one classmate’s video.
  • Provide thoughtful, constructive feedback.

This is your last step in Unit 4.

Activity A – Self-Reflection (6–8 Sentences)

Write a short reflection that looks back on your experience creating your video. Aim for about 6–8 complete sentences.

Suggested prompts:

  • What are you proudest of in your final video? Why?
  • Describe one challenge you faced during planning, filming, or editing.
  • Which piece of RISE feedback (from 4.4) helped you the most, and what did you change because of it?
  • What specific editing or storytelling skill improved the most for you?
  • If you had more time, what is one thing you would improve or add?
  • How does your final video compare to the earlier version you shared for RISE feedback?

You may type your reflection in a Word document or directly into the 4.6 assignment in Teams.

Activity B – Complete the Showcase Submission Template

When you hand in your final video in Teams, you will also complete a Showcase Submission Template. This makes it easy to add your project to our class gallery.

Template fields:

  • Project Title
  • 1-Sentence Hook / Tagline
    A short line that makes people want to watch.
  • Short Description (2–3 sentences)
    Explain what your video is about, who it is for, and the main idea.
  • Alt Text (1–2 sentences)
    Describe what a screen reader should say about your thumbnail image.
  • Credits
    Music source and license, plus first names of any collaborators. (e.g. "Music by Artist Name, licensed under XYZ", who helped with filming, etc.)

You will fill this out as part of your final assignment in Teams - copy the information and format it using your Style Guide.

Hand-In – 4.6 Reflection & Showcase Submission Document

For this lesson, you will submit the following in the 4.6 assignment on Teams:

  • Self-Reflection: 6–8 sentences responding to the prompts above.
  • Showcase Submission Template

Together with your final video and showcase submission, these pieces complete your assessment for Unit 4 – Video Production for the Web.
