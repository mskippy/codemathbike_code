Overview

In this lesson, you’ll take your initial video idea and turn it into a clear plan. By the end, you’ll have a complete pre-production package ready for teacher approval.

Learning Goals

  • Develop a clear concept and 30–60 second pitch for a short web video.
  • Choose an appropriate structure (tutorial, mini-documentary, comedy, PSA, etc.) for your idea.
  • Create a basic script and storyboard that show what will happen in your video.
  • Build a shot list that outlines what you need to film.
  • Plan practical details like location, permissions, and basic lighting.

1. Warm-Up — What Makes a Clear Concept?

In pairs or small groups:

  1. Pick one short online video (tutorial, PSA, or mini-doc).
  2. Answer these questions in your notebook or on OneNote:
    • What is the main idea of the video (in one sentence)?
    • Who is the target audience?
    • How is the video structured (beginning, middle, end)?
  3. Post your example and a short summary to the class Padlet below.

3. Your Task — Build Your Pre-Production Package

Your goal is to create a complete pre-production package for your Unit 4 video. You must get teacher approval before you start filming.

This is an individual assignment - you must come up with your own video pitch and production package. You cannot collaborate with others or share the same idea.

A. Project Pitch (30–60 seconds)

Create a brief pitch that clearly explains your idea. You can present it live in class or record it as a short video or audio clip.

Your pitch should include:

  • Title of your video (working title is fine).
  • Type of video: tutorial, mini-doc, comedy, PSA, or other (explain).
  • Big idea in one sentence (What is your video about?).
  • Audience: Who are you making this for?
  • Goal: What do you want your audience to think, feel, or do after watching?

B. Script

Write a basic script that covers your whole video.

  • Include key dialogue, voiceover, or narration.
  • Note important sound effects or music ideas.
  • Keep it concise and realistic for your time limit.

C. Storyboard

Use the storyboard template (or your own layout) to map out the main shots of your video.

  • Aim for 8–16 panels depending on your idea.
  • Sketch what the camera will see in each shot.
  • Add notes about action, camera angle, and audio.

D. Shot List

Create a shot list that you can bring with you on filming days.

  • Number each shot.
  • Include: shot type (wide, medium, close-up), location, who is in it, and any special notes.
  • Group shots by location to make filming more efficient.

E. Production Planning Checklist

Answer these questions in your planning document:

  • Locations: Where will you film? (Classroom, hallway, outdoors, etc.)
  • Permissions: Do you need anyone’s permission to film here or to film them?
  • Schedule: When will you film? Who needs to be there?
  • Props & costumes: What items do you need to bring?
  • Lighting: Will you use natural light, classroom lights, or extra lights? Any test shots needed?

Elevator Pitch – Share Your Video Idea

Once you’ve completed your full pre-production package (pitch, script, storyboard, and shot list), you will create a short elevator pitch to share your final video concept with the class. This pitch is a quick summary that highlights the key ideas from your planning.

What to Include in Your Pitch

  • Title of your video
  • The main idea in one sentence
  • Your target audience (who the video is for)
  • What the viewer will learn or feel
  • Why this video matters or what makes it interesting

Example:
"My video, How to Land a Bunny Hop, will teach beginner mountain bikers the three key steps to safely lift both wheels off the ground. It’s fast-paced, visual, and encourages new riders to build confidence on the trail."

4. Group Work After Pitches (Optional)

Once you have completed your individual pitch, you may choose to work in a small group (2–3 people) with classmates whose ideas connect to yours. Grouping is optional, but collaboration can make filming more efficient and more fun.

How Grouping Works

  • Everyone must first create and submit their own pitch.
  • After pitches are approved, you may form a group based on:
    • Similar topics
    • Compatible concepts
    • Shared style or purpose (e.g., PSA, tutorial, mini-documentary)
  • Groups may plan together, share locations, and support each other during filming.

Important: You Still Produce Your Own Video

Even in a group, each person must create and submit their own project, including:

  • Your own script
  • Your own storyboard
  • Your own shot list
  • Your own edited final video

Your group may create:

  • A connected series (e.g., Episode 1, Episode 2…)
  • Different perspectives on the same topic
  • Similar videos that support a shared message

But everyone is responsible for their own creative work.

What Groups Can Share

  • Brainstorming and refining ideas
  • Research
  • Locations (with permission)
  • Props and equipment
  • Helping each other film shots (acting or assisting)

What Must Be Individual

Each student must submit their own:

  • Pitch
  • Pre-production package
  • Final video

This ensures everyone demonstrates their individual skills in planning, filming, and editing.

5. Teacher Approval Before Filming

Before you can sign out equipment or start filming, you must show your teacher the following:

  • Pitch (spoken or recorded) clearly explaining your idea and audience.
  • Script for the full video.
  • Storyboard with clear visual planning.
  • Shot list organized by location.
  • Production planning checklist with locations, permissions, schedule, and any special considerations.

Once your pre-production package is approved, you can move on to filming in Lesson 4.3.

7. Reflection

After completing your pre-production package, respond to the reflection prompt below. Add your thoughts directly to the class Padlet.

  1. What part of the pre-production process helped you understand your idea the most (pitch, script, storyboard, or shot list)? Why?
  2. What are you most excited about filming?
  3. What challenges do you anticipate, and how can you plan for them now?
