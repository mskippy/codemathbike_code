Overview

Big idea: Package your video so it can be shared on our class gallery page in a clear, accessible, and professional way.

Instead of each student building a separate webpage, we will create a shared Class Video Showcase Page on the course website. Your job is to submit all the information needed to display your video properly.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Provide a clear title, hook, and short description for your video.
  • Write effective alt text for your thumbnail.
  • Prepare a thumbnail image file.
  • Share your video file or link in a way that can be embedded.
  • Confirm ethical and accessibility checks before publishing.

Activity A – Complete the Showcase Submission Template

When you hand in your final video in Teams, you will also complete a Showcase Submission Template. This makes it easy to add your project to our class gallery.

Template fields:

  • Project Title
  • 1-Sentence Hook / Tagline
    A short line that makes people want to watch.
  • Short Description (2–3 sentences)
    Explain what your video is about, who it is for, and the main idea.
  • Alt Text (1–2 sentences)
    Describe what a screen reader should say about your thumbnail image.
  • Thumbnail File Name
    LastName_ProjectTitle_Thumbnail.png
  • Video File or Link Location
    Where your video is (Teams upload, OneDrive link, or YouTube link).
  • Credits
    Music source and license, plus first names of any collaborators.
  • Permission Check
    Confirm that everyone in the video agreed to appear on camera.

You will fill this out as part of your final assignment in Teams - copy the information and format it using your Style Guide.

Activity B – Thumbnail & Description Polish

Before you submit, make sure your thumbnail and description look professional and support your video.

Thumbnail Requirements

  • Size around 1280×720 px (YouTube-style).
  • Uses a clear still frame or graphic from your video.
  • Includes short, readable text (4–6 words max).
  • Has strong contrast between text and background.
  • Follows copyright rules (original or properly licensed images only).

Description Requirements

  • 2–3 sentences, written for a viewer who has not seen your work yet.
  • Uses first names only (no last names).
  • Explains the main idea or story and why it matters.

Activity C – Ethics & Accessibility Check

Before your project appears on the class gallery page, complete this quick checklist:

  • ✔ Everyone in the video has given permission to appear.
  • ✔ Only first names are used (no last names or personal contact details).
  • ✔ No sensitive personal information is visible in the video.
  • ✔ Music is original or Creative Commons with proper credits.
  • ✔ Captions are turned on if your hosting platform supports them.
  • ✔ Alt text is written for your thumbnail image.

This helps keep our class gallery safe, respectful, and accessible for all viewers.
