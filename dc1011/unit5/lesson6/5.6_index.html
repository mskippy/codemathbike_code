Overview

Big idea: Your website is live. This final step is about making sure it is complete, functional, and ready to share — and then reflecting on what you learned this semester.

This page shows what “finished” looks like and what you need to submit.

Part 1 — Final Self-Check (Before You Submit)

Use this checklist to make sure your site is showcase-ready. If you can check everything below, you’re in good shape.

This is the checklist that will be used to assess your work.

A. Live & Working

  • ☐ My live website link opens (no errors)
  • ☐ My homepage loads first
  • ☐ All navigation links work
  • ☐ My site is styled (CSS is loading)
  • ☐ Images and media load correctly

B. Required Pages & Content

  • ☐ At least 4 pages (Home, About, Gallery/Projects, Contact or equivalent)
  • ☐ All text is my own writing
  • ☐ Content is written for the web (headings, short paragraphs)
  • ☐ Gallery/Projects page shows real work or examples
  • ☐ Contact page is school-appropriate and intentional

C. Structure & Organization

  • ☐ Navigation is consistent on every page
  • ☐ File names are lowercase and have no spaces
  • ☐ Files and folders are organized
  • ☐ Nothing feels broken or unfinished

D. Design & Personal Branding

This is about ownership, not perfection.

  • ☐ Colours are customized (not default template)
  • ☐ Fonts were chosen intentionally
  • ☐ Spacing/layout has been adjusted
  • ☐ Visual style matches my planned “vibe”
  • ☐ The site feels like mine, not just the starter template

E. Final Polish

  • ☐ No empty sections or placeholder text
  • ☐ Text is easy to read
  • ☐ Colours have good contrast
  • ☐ I would be comfortable sharing this publicly
  • ☐ My teacher can add my link directly to the showcase

Part 2 — Capstone Reflection

This website is your capstone project for the semester. The reflection is about skills, growth, and transfer — not just the final product.

Write ~200–300 words answering all of the following:

  1. What is one skill from this unit that you feel confident using now?
  2. How did you move beyond the starter template and make your site your own?
  3. Describe one challenge you faced and how you worked through it.
  4. How could you use what you learned in this project outside this class?
  5. If you had more time, what is one improvement or feature you would add?

Include one screenshot of your GitHub repo folder in your reflection.

Submit on Teams

What to Submit

  • ☐ Reflection (with repo screenshot included)
  • ☐ Live website link (GitHub Pages URL)

This submit list will also be posted in Teams — use it to double-check before you click Turn in.

Final Check

If your site works without explanation, your design feels intentional, and your reflection is complete — you’re done.

Nice work finishing the unit strong.
