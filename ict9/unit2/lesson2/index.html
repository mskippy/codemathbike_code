Intro

Now it is time to create Platforms for our Player to run on.

  • Ground platforms = solid floors/walls the player cannot pass through.
  • Floating platforms = smaller “islands” placed at various heights.
  • Jump-thru (one-way) = player can jump up through them from below and land on top (we’ll prep the art now, add behavior later).

1. Turn On Grid & Snapping

  1. Select the Toggle/Grid<> → Show grid<> (ON).
  2. Select Setup grid<> to set grid the grid size to match your tiles: 64 × 64.

    3. Why: snapping keeps edges aligned so collisions are clean and movement feels right.

2. Add the Tilemap for Platforms

  1. Add a new object<> → choose Tile map<> → select Skip and create from scratch<>
    • Object name: Level 1 Platforms
    • Tile size: 64
    • Choose a file: File(s) from your device U2 Game Development<> → assets<> → Tilesheet<> → tilesheet_complete.png<>
    • In the Editor click on the tiles that will be used as platforms - it will configure its behaviours.
  2. Click Apply<>.
  3. Drag the Level_1_Platforms<> object to the scene layout and click on it. You'll notice the Tilemap painter<> is now open. Enter 0 for both the X<> and Y<> values.
  4. Close the Layers<> and Objects<> pannel to adjust the view so you can see more of the Tilemap painter

We are not placing individual tiles as separate objects — we paint everything inside this one Tilemap.

3 "Painting" Platforms

You'll notice that you can click on a tile and it is selected. At the same time the paint brush button is selected. This means you will "draw" this tile on your layout.

  • Place your cursor where you want to draw your platform, hold your button down and the tile will be drawn.
  • Draw some platforms. Combine different tiles to form different grounds. You can spend a lot of time creating your game world, but for now create enough to help you learn how to make your game.
  • You can create a platform or two and duplicate them for faster building
  • Make platforms to span the whole of your background.

Here is an example you can copy if you don't know where to start.

4. Lock & Behaviours

  1. Click on the Open Instance List Panel<> and lock all the Level_1_Platforms<> objects.
  2. Open Objects menu and double-click on the Level_1_Platforms<> object.
  3. Click on the Behaviors tab and Add a behavior<>.
  4. Add the Platform<> behavior. This will make your platform a solid object for sprites to run along.
  5. Click Apply.
  6. Save<> your work

5. Floating Platforms

Now let's add some floating platforms. These are platforms that the sprite can jump through to get on top of.

  1. Right click on the Level_1_Platforms<> object and duplicate it.
  2. Rename the object to Level_1_Floating<>
  3. Double click the Level_1_Floating<> object and change the Platform<> behviour, to Jumpthrough platform<>.
  4. Drag the object onto the layout and position it at X : 0 and Y : 0, and double click on it to open the Tilemap painter.
  5. Create one or two floating platforms. Select and duplicate to make multiples easily.
  6. Open the Instance List Panel<> and lock all the floating objects.
  7. Save your work.

6. Preview & Save

  1. Click Preview<> to check visuals (movement won’t work yet—no player).
  2. Look for gaps/misaligned edges. Fix anything that isn’t snapping cleanly to the grid. (Tiles will snap to the grid if it is viewable)
  3. Save your project (Ctrl + S).
Troubleshooting tips
  • Edges look fuzzy? Use whole-number sizes aligned to grid; avoid fractional scaling.
  • Texture repeats oddly? Ensure your source tile is exactly 64×64 (or your chosen grid size).

7. Reflection

Think about the choices you made when designing your platforms.

  • How did the placement of your ground and floating platforms affect how a player might move through your level?
  • What challenges or strategies do you think your platform layout creates for the player?
  • If you were to redesign your level, what would you change to make it easier or harder?

Note on Hit Boxes (Collision Polygons)

Every object in your game has an invisible hit box (collision polygon) that the engine uses to detect when things touch. For platforms built with the Tilemap + Collision Mask<>, these are handled automatically — you do not need to configure them now.

Later, when we add the Player and Enemies, we will adjust their hit boxes so collisions feel accurate (for example, aligning the player’s hit box to their body, not the empty space around the sprite).

For now, you can ignore hit box editing and focus on building your level layout.
