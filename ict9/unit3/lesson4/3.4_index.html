Overview

Big idea: Apply your game’s branding to create a polished Game Poster (.PNG) and Website Banner (.PNG) to use in your marketing page.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Combine your logo, colours, fonts, and characters into a cohesive layout.
  • Use visual hierarchy to guide the viewer’s eye.
  • Design both a full-page poster and a website banner that match your style guide.
  • Export high-quality PNG files ready to upload to your website.

You should have completed: 3.1 Understanding Promotion, 3.2 Game Branding, and 3.3 Style Guide.

Activity A – Game Poster (.PNG)

  1. Start a new Canva design.
    Use a vertical layout such as Letter (8.5 × 11") or a Poster template.
  2. Plan your visual hierarchy.
    Make sure:
    • Your game title or logo is the largest and easiest thing to read.
    • Your main character, scene, or screenshot draws attention next.
    • A short tagline supports the mood/genre of the game.
    • The background supports the design but doesn’t overpower it.
  3. Apply your branding.
    Use your style guide as a checklist:
    • Brand colours are used consistently.
    • Heading and body fonts match your guide.
    • Logo is placed clearly but not too large.
    • Characters, icons, and screenshots reflect your game world.
  4. Export your poster.
    • File → Download → PNG.
    • File name: GameName_Poster.png

Activity B – Website Banner (.PNG)

  1. Create a wide canvas in Canva.
    Use a banner layout such as:
    • 1920 × 600 px, or
    • Canva’s built-in Website Banner template.
  2. Keep it simple and readable.
    • Place your logo or title clearly (left or centre).
    • Add one main visual (character, icon, or screenshot).
    • Use a very short tagline or no extra text at all.
    • Make sure text has strong contrast with the background.
  3. Export your banner.
    • File → Download → PNG.
    • File name: GameName_WebBanner.png

Choose Your Challenge – Optional Poster Power-Ups

If you finish early or want to push your design further, choose at least one challenge below. These are optional extensions to your main poster and banner, not new required projects.

Polish and Refine (entry-level extensions)

Focus on improving your existing poster and banner without starting from scratch.

  • Alternate colour version: Create a second copy of your poster with a different colour scheme (warm vs cool, light vs dark) and compare the mood.
  • Minimalist version: Simplify your poster using fewer elements, more empty space, and only the most important details.
  • Typography tune-up: Adjust font sizes, spacing, and alignment so everything is easy to read at a distance.
  • Portrait vs landscape: Rebuild your poster in the opposite orientation (if it was vertical, try a horizontal version).
New Poster Perspective (intermediate)

Create a fresh version of your poster from a different point of view.

  • Character point-of-view: Design a scene as if the viewer is seeing through the main character’s eyes, or with the character in the foreground looking toward the action.
  • Key moment poster: Freeze one intense gameplay moment (a chase, boss battle, or power-up) and design the poster around that scene.
  • Character spotlight: Make a poster that focuses on a single hero or main character with a short character tagline.
  • Environment/world-building: Highlight the game world (forest, city, space station, etc.) instead of the character.
Advanced Marketing Designs (stretch)

Try these if you are confident with Canva and want to experiment with more complex marketing-style designs.

  • Cinematic movie-style poster: Add film-style credits, a release date, and a dramatic composition that looks like a movie poster.
  • Enemy or boss reveal: Create a poster that introduces the main villain or threat, with a warning or dramatic tagline.
  • Social media promo set: Adapt your design to two extra formats such as an Instagram square, Instagram story, or YouTube thumbnail.
  • Animated poster or motion banner: Use Canva’s animation tools to make a subtle animated version (glow, particles, movement). Still export a PNG, but you can also keep the MP4 or GIF for your website later.
