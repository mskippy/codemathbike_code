3.3 Character & Visual Asset Creation

Goal: Create a main game character and 1–2 supporting visual assets for use in your game and your Game Promotion Package.

In this lesson, you will:

  • Design a simple, vector-based main character in Canva.
  • Create 1–2 supporting assets (icons, power-ups, hearts, coins, etc.).
  • Export everything as both SVG (editable) and PNG (ready to use in posters or websites).

3. Design Your Main Character in Canva

Open Canva and create a new design (around 1024 × 1024 px works well). Your character should be simple and easy to read.

Your character must:

  • Be made with vector shapes only (no photos).
  • Use a consistent colour palette that fits your game’s tone and genre.
  • Have a clear style (flat or outlined) and a readable silhouette.
  • Match the audience and mood of your game (serious, cute, spooky, sci-fi, etc.).

Examples of main characters:

  • A platformer hero or mascot.
  • A creature, robot, or alien.
  • A fantasy character (mage, archer, warrior).
  • A sports player or futuristic explorer.

4. Create 1–2 Supporting Visual Assets

Next, design a few small assets that match your character’s style. These will be helpful in your poster and other promotion materials.

Ideas for supporting assets:

  • Collectibles: coins, gems, stars, tickets.
  • Health or protection: hearts, shields, battery bars.
  • Power-ups: speed boost, jump token, magic orb, special key.
  • UI icons: menu button, pause icon, settings symbol.

Keep them consistent:

  • Use the same colours as your main character.
  • Match the same line thickness and style (flat vs outlined).
  • Make sure they look like they belong in the same world.

5. Export Your Character and Assets

You must export each asset twice:

  • SVG – keeps your shapes editable (vector).
  • PNG – ready to place in your poster or website.

Export steps in Canva:

  1. Select your character or asset.
  2. Click Share → Download.
  3. Choose SVG as the file type and download.
  4. Repeat and download the same asset as a PNG.

File naming examples:

  • firstname_character.svg
  • firstname_character.png
  • firstname_asset1.svg
  • firstname_asset1.png
  • firstname_asset2.svg
  • firstname_asset2.png

6. Add Screenshots to Your Character Sheet

While you work, take screenshots and paste them into your Word document.

Include screenshots of:

  • Your Canva workspace showing your character.
  • The supporting assets on the canvas.
  • Your colour palette or shapes (if visible).
  • (Optional) The export/download settings window.

Make sure each screenshot is labelled clearly so it’s easy to understand what you are showing.
