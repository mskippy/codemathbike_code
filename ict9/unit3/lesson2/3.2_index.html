Overview

Create a visual identity for your game that communicates tone and personality. You’ll learn the four core design principles — CRAP (Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, Proximity) — and apply them to design your logo, choose your colors, and write a tagline. Everything comes together in a one-page Game Brand Style Guide you’ll make in Canva.

Deliverable: Game Brand Style Guide

Activity A — Mini-Lesson: Design Principles 101

Watch the video on CRAP principles. Notice how each principle guides attention and mood.

Key Ideas

  • Contrast: Make important elements stand out (color, size, weight).
  • Repetition: Repeat colors, fonts, or shapes for unity.
  • Alignment: Line things up to reduce visual noise.
  • Proximity: Group related items so they read as one unit.

Quick Examples

  • Bright title on a dark background (contrast).
  • Same stroke/shape style repeated across letters (repetition).
  • Centered title for stability; left-aligned for a modern feel (alignment).
  • Title + tagline kept close so they read together (proximity).

Activity C — Create Your Game Brand Style Guide (in Canva)

Design your own brand identity and collect everything on a single page.

Steps

  1. Open Canva → create a blank 8.5 × 11 in page (portrait).
  2. Top section: Add your game logo and tagline.
  3. Color palette: Add 3–5 swatches and label each with its HEX code.
  4. Typography: Show your chosen font(s) and list their font names.
  5. Connections & Reflection: Write 1–2 sentences explaining:
    • Why these colors and fonts fit your game’s tone, genre, or story.
    • How your design supports the player experience.
  6. Export your page as a high-quality PNG or PDF.
