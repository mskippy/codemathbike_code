Overview

Big idea: Bring your game assets together to create a short, engaging Game Trailer (.MP4) that shows off your world, gameplay, and branding.

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Plan a storyboard that highlights the most important parts of your game.
  • Capture short gameplay clips that match your plan.
  • Combine clips, music, titles, and your logo/tagline into a polished trailer.
  • Export your trailer as a .MP4 file ready to share on your website.

You should have completed: 3.1 Understanding Promotion, 3.2 Game Branding, 3.3 Style Guide, and 3.4 Poster & Banner Design.

Activity A – Plan Your Trailer (Storyboard)

  1. Decide the length.
    Aim for a trailer that is about 30–60 seconds long.
  2. Create 5–8 “shots”.
    On paper or in Word, draw or list 5–8 boxes. For each box, decide:
    • Visual: What will we see? (menu, level, boss, power-up, etc.)
    • Text: What words appear on screen, if any?
    • Audio: Music or important sound effects.
  3. Include a clear beginning, middle, and end.
    Think about:
    • Opening hook: How do you grab attention in the first few seconds?
    • Gameplay moments: How will you show what it feels like to play?
    • Ending: Logo and tagline reveal, with a final “hook” or question.
  4. Check your tone and branding.
    Make sure your storyboard matches your game’s genre and mood (cute, spooky, epic, chaotic, etc.).

Activity B – Capture Gameplay Clips

  1. Practice your moves.
    Play your game a few times so you can smoothly show:
    • Your main mechanic (jumping, shooting, dodging, etc.).
    • At least one challenge moment.
    • At least one success moment (winning, completing a level, grabbing a big reward).
  2. Use the recording tool shown in class.
    For example, using Xbox Game Bar:
    • Press Win + G to open the overlay.
    • Use the capture controls to start and stop recording.
  3. Record short clips, not one long video.
    Try to keep each clip between 5–15 seconds so you can easily rearrange them later.
  4. Organize your files.
    Save your clips in a folder such as H:\GamePromotion\Trailer_Clips<> or in a clearly labelled OneDrive folder.

Activity C – Edit Your Trailer

  1. Create a new project in your editor.
    Open Clipchamp (or the editor your teacher has shown) and create a project called GameName_Trailer.
  2. Import your assets.
    Bring in:
    • Your gameplay clips
    • Your logo and any key art (poster, banner, character art)
    • Your music track (if allowed for school use)
  3. Build the timeline.
    • Arrange clips in the same order as your storyboard.
    • Trim the start/end of clips to remove “dead time”.
    • Use simple transitions (cuts, fades). Avoid overusing wild effects.
  4. Add titles and text.
    Include:
    • A short opening title or hook.
    • Optional text overlays such as “Dodge enemies”, “Collect power-ups”, “Beat the boss”.
    • A final screen with your logo and tagline.
  5. Balance the sound.
    Turn music down if it’s too loud, and make sure important sound effects can still be heard.
  6. Export your trailer.
    • Export as .MP4 at a reasonable quality setting.
    • File name: LastName_FirstName_GameName_Trailer.mp4<>

Reflection & Hand-In Checklist

When you are ready to hand in your work in Teams, make sure you have:

  • Game Trailer: LastName_FirstName_GameName_Trailer.mp4<>
  • Short reflection paragraph (Word document or typed in Teams) that explains:
    • What feeling or mood you tried to create with your trailer.
    • How you chose which gameplay moments to show.
    • How your trailer connects to your logo, colours, and poster.
    • One thing that worked well and one thing you would improve.

Attach your trailer and reflection to the Teams assignment for 3.5 Game Trailer Production.
