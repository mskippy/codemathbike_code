Overview

In this lesson, you’ll create an About Me page in Canva that shows who you are using good design choices, not just random colours and fonts.

You’ll choose a Canva template you like, customize it with your own information, and use design principles to make your poster clear, readable, and visually appealing.

Learning goals

  • Use Canva to create and edit a one-page design.
  • Apply basic design principles: contrast, alignment, hierarchy, spacing, colour & fonts.
  • Choose fonts, colours, and images that support your message.
  • Explain how your design choices affect how others read your poster.
  • Export and submit your Canva work correctly to Teams.

Your task: Canva “About Me” page

You will create a single-page About Me poster in Canva. Instead of starting from scratch, you will choose a template that already has a strong design, then customize it to match your personality.

Step 1 – Choose your template

  1. Sign in to Canva with your school account.
  2. Search for a template using keywords like:
    • poster, profile, about me, aesthetic poster, simple layout, minimalist
  3. Pick a template you like the look of (your aesthetic):
    • Calm / minimalist
    • Bold / colourful
    • Modern / clean
    • Cute / playful
  4. Make sure the template already shows good design (clear title, good spacing, readable text).

Step 2 – Customize with your own content

Your About Me page must include:

  • Your name (first name is fine).
  • A photo or avatar that represents you (school appropriate).
  • 3–5 “About Me” facts (hobbies, interests, favourite subjects, activities).
  • 2–3 favourites (food, game, show, book, sport, etc.).
  • At least 2 icons or images from Canva Elements that relate to you.

Replace the template’s placeholder text, images, and colours with your own choices. Use short phrases or bullet points instead of long paragraphs.

Step 3 – Apply the design principles

As you edit your template, check that you are using each design principle:

  • Contrast: Is the text easy to read against the background?
  • Alignment: Do your text boxes and images line up?
  • Hierarchy: Is your title the most noticeable text?
  • Spacing: Is there space between sections so it doesn’t look crowded?
  • Colour & fonts: Are you using 2–3 colours and 1–2 fonts consistently?

Step 4 – Final polish & export

  • Remove any unused text boxes or shapes from the original template.
  • Check for spelling and basic punctuation.
  • Make sure everything is school appropriate.
  • Export your design as a PNG file and save it to your H: drive
How to save your Canva design as a PNG
  1. Finish your design
    Check your text, images, colours, and layout. Make sure everything looks the way you want.
  2. Click the “Share” button
    In Canva, look at the top-right corner and click the purple Share button.
  3. Choose “Download”
    In the menu that opens, click Download.
  4. Set the file type to PNG
    In the File type dropdown, choose PNG.
    (If it says PDF or JPEG, click it and switch it to PNG.)
  5. Choose pages (if there is more than one)
    If your design has multiple pages, choose Just this page or select the page you want to download.
  6. Click the purple “Download” button
    Canva will prepare your file and then your PNG will download to your computer (usually into your Downloads folder).
  7. Rename your file
    Go to your Downloads folder and rename the file using our class naming format, for example:
    2.1_AboutMe_FirstnameLastname.png<>
  8. Move it to your ICT 8 folder
    Move the file into your ICT8 → Unit 2 folder on your H: drive (or OneDrive, if you are using that).

Reflection

On a separate document in Canva or Word, answer the following questions about your design choices:

A. Reflection questions (2–3 sentences)

  1. What design choice are you most proud of? Why?
  2. What is one thing you would improve if you had more time?

B. Design principles checklist

Explain how you used each design principle in your About Me poster. One short sentence per item is enough.

  • Contrast: How did you make your text easy to read?
  • Alignment: How did you line things up?
  • Hierarchy: How did you show what’s most important?
  • Spacing: How did you avoid crowding your layout?
  • Colour & fonts: How did you keep your colours and fonts consistent?
