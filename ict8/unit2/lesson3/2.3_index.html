Overview

In this activity, you’ll learn how to remove unwanted objects or “photobombers” from a picture using simple, free digital tools. You will choose one of two methods:

  • Canva Magic Eraser (fast and beginner-friendly)
  • Photopea Clone/Healing Tools (more advanced, Photoshop-style)

Your goal is to clean up a real photo and create a fun before/after comparison that you’ll share on our class Padlet gallery.

Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Identify unwanted elements in a photo.
  • Use digital tools to erase or hide them.
  • Make simple touch-ups to blend the background.
  • Export and share edited images in a digital gallery.

Mini Lesson: What Is a Photobomber?

A photobomber is anything that appears in your picture that shouldn’t be there:

  • A random person in the background
  • A bird flying through at the wrong moment
  • A person making a funny face behind you
  • A car, a hand, or even a shadow

Photo editing tools can help remove these distractions so your final picture looks clean and intentional.

Ethical Note

Editing photos can be fun and creative, but remember:

  • Don’t alter photos in misleading ways.
  • Don’t edit real people without their permission.
  • Use your skills responsibly and respectfully.

This activity is just for practice—not for altering important, personal, or official images.

Choose Your Challenge

Pick ONE of the two workflows below. Both will help you learn essential photo-editing skills.

Option A — Canva “Fast Fix” Method (Beginner-Friendly)

You’ll Use:

  • Canva Magic Eraser
  • Optional: Blur tools, background adjust, stickers/text

Steps:

  1. Find Your Photo
    Choose a picture that has something in it you want to remove (you can use a provided class photo or your own).
  2. Upload to Canva
    Open Canva → Create a Custom Size design → Upload your image.
  3. Use Magic Eraser
    • Select the photo
    • Choose Edit Photo → Magic Eraser
    • Brush over the photobomber
    • Let Canva remove it automatically
  4. Clean Up the Area
    • Zoom in
    • Use the eraser again if needed
    • Optional: add blur to soften messy edges
  5. Add Fun Elements (Optional)
    • Stickers
    • Comic-style text
    • A caption
    • A frame
  6. Export Your File
    Download as PNG.
  7. Save Your Before/After
    Keep your original image and your edited image for posting to Padlet.

Option B — Photopea “Pro Edit” Method (Advanced)

You’ll Use:

  • Photopea (https://www.photopea.com/)
  • Spot Healing Brush
  • Clone Stamp Tool
  • Layers

Steps:

  1. Open Your Photo
    Go to Photopea → File → Open.
  2. Duplicate the Layer
    Always work on a copy. Right-click → Duplicate Layer.
  3. Choose a Removal Tool
    • Spot Healing Brush for quick fixes
    • Clone Stamp for detailed work
    • Patch Tool for larger areas
  4. Zoom In and Erase the Photobomber
    Work slowly and blend the background. Sample from clean areas to patch over messy parts.
  5. Refine Your Edges
    • Switch brush sizes
    • Use soft brushes for blending
    • Undo if something looks off
  6. Export Your File
    File → Export As → PNG.
  7. Save Your Before/After
    Keep both versions for posting to Padlet.
